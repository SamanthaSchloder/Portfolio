Course Goals


1. Consider technology’s role in acts of making

Prior to this class I didn’t really understand how digital media was effectively done. I had heard of Adobe and it seemed so unreachable and outside of my skill set. From this class I realize that every design goes more smoothly with a reference and that software like Adobe is easy once you learn the basics. Technology is there for you to use if you choose to.

2. Embrace experimentation

I have learned that Control + Z is my best friend during experimentation. Trying new things with a design can get freaky but there is always a chance that it may work. When it does work, it can take your design to a whole new level.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment

For each of these projects there was a show and tell time during class where the individual was able to get feedback on their creation. I found this workshop environment extremely beneficial. Not only did I get feedback from the professor and my peers, but I also had the opportunity to see what my peers created. It was interesting to see the creative process of other people and how they utilized the tools in Adobe in maybe a different way. Overall workshopping was a great way to learn more about digital media and get more effective in Adobe.

4. Practice revision within and across media forms

The opportunity for revision is one of the reasons why I enjoy this class so much. Nothing is ever final, and it can always be improved and changed. I revised my designs multiple times while working and I think that is why I am so proud of these works.

5. Learn to present your work

For each project there was a presentation aspect that became easier the more we did it. Although I continue to be nervous about the quality of my work from time to time just because it seems that everyone in the class is giving it their all.

Course Goals

1. Consider technology’s role in acts of making

Through this creative process I have found that technology can have a huge impact on photos. By utilizing the tools in Lightroom Classic a photo can look completely different from when it started. This tool creates some amazing works, but a part of me also believes it might warp reality.


2. Embrace experimentation

The tools in Lightroom Classic opened a whole new way to edit photos for me. I loved being able to play around with every scale. For each picture I would see how it changed if I moved from one side of the scale to the other, experimenting with the extremes. Sometimes I would like it, and sometimes I would hate it, that was the fun.


3.Engage with others in a workshop environment

While I don’t always like it, I would like to hear more constructive criticism from my peers. When we broke out to discuss our photo essays all they gave me were comments on what they liked. While this did boost my ego, it didn’t help me figure out what I could do better. I will make more of an effort to give my peers constructive criticism on their works so that maybe they will do the same when I present my work.


4. Practice revision within and across media forms

Can the editing process of a picture ever be finished? I find it difficult to find a stopping point when revising my photos. I keep wanting to change it and I am never fully satisfied. Photography is one skill that I do not want to master. It is such an intricate art I feel that it takes a lot of time and dedication to train your eye to be a good photographer, and this is something I am not too interested in.


5. Learn to present your work

No matter how many times we seem to do it, I am always nervous about showing my work to others. This comes across in all artforms. I don’t know why I am so scared but taking this leap each time is never easy. Although I have found that it is helpful if I write out a list of what I want to say when presenting my work.

Course Goals


1. Consider technology’s role in acts of making

One of the major pieces of technology used to make podcasts is the microphone. At first, I used my computer’s microphone to try and pick up sound. These recordings didn’t really sound all that great. Upon further experimentation I found that if I used my roommate’s gaming microphone, I could obtain a much better sound that made a better podcast overall. This section on podcasting taught me that not all microphones are the same. There are some microphones that work for a phone call or a zoom meeting, but don’t work so well for a quality podcast.

2. Embrace experimentation

Sound is a tricky beast that requires a lot of patience and experimentation. Choosing music to add to each podcast was my favorite part of this design process. It was fun trying to pick the best sounds that matched the mood I was attempting to convey. Sometimes it worked, and sometimes it didn’t.

3.Engage with others in a workshop environment

It makes me more comfortable now that we split up into smaller groups and show our work. Similar with other projects I get so nervous showing everyone in the class and it is less of a worry when only 2 or 3 people listen in. It also gave me the confidence to show my second podcast to the whole group, which was nice.

4. Practice revision within and across media forms

I found it more challenging to practice revision in this section. I find myself really wanting to go in and add other elements to the podcasts as I think of them, but I am lacking the time. The past three weeks have been a combination of a lot of different projects and I needed to dedicate my time elsewhere.

5. Learn to present your work

Presenting my podcasts has hit me a bit differently than the other media forms. When I present my work, I listen to it with my audience to gauge when it is over and when my peers are ready to talk. When I listen to it again in class it has normally been a little while since I recorded and mixed it together, so I get sort of a removed viewpoint of the podcast and I see it differently. I pick up on things I didn’t notice before and things I like about it. p>

Course Goals


1. Consider technology’s role in acts of making

I have always wanted to make videos for some reason or another but found it challenging to produce what I wanted until I learned how to use Adobe’s Premiere Pro. My first attempt was before I had extra storage available for the program to run on and it ended in several failed attempts. Understanding how the program works by running on your computer helped keep the program from crashing. Creating these videos was quite satisfying and I am so glad that I have learned this new skill.


2. Embrace experimentation

It took me a while to become comfortable with layering objects in this software. I don’t know why I was so against it, but now I find this sort of experimentation my favorite. Playing with position, text, opacity, and movement can do so many cool and exciting things to my work.


3.Engage with others in a workshop environment

Like with all the other projects, the time we had to workshop gave me the opportunity to see what everyone else created and learn from their work. I really enjoyed being in a small group and getting feedback from my peers.


4. Practice revision within and across media forms

I understand how important revision is and again with this section on film making I don’t think I took the opportunity to practice revision enough. After showing my work in the workshop I found it challenging to put the time aside to take the comments I received and make my films better.


5. Learn to present your work

During this class we have presented our work numerous times and I can now conclude that I will never like this aspect of creation. Waiting for the reaction of people as they listen or watch my work is nerve wrecking. I have thought about drinking more tea, but no amount seems to calm me down enough.
